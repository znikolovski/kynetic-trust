# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Two overlapping audiences sharing a single entry point. **Primary**: high-net-worth individuals — sophisticated private clients who hold real capital and are looking for institutional-grade access without the gatekeeping of traditional private banking. They are results-oriented, impatient with friction, and read financial data fluently. **Secondary**: institutional buyers — fund managers, family office treasurers, and corporate treasury desks evaluating SecurBank for structured products, credit facilities, or liquidity management. They have dealt with incumbent banks and know exactly what they are comparing against. The site must earn both audiences simultaneously: approachable enough for the HNW individual who arrived from a referral, rigorous enough for the institutional buyer who will scrutinise the rate sheet.

## Product Purpose

The SecurBank marketing site presents the full product range — credit cards, mortgages, savings accounts, and investment services — to prospective clients who have not yet signed up. It exists to convert qualified interest into product exploration, and product exploration into an application. Success looks like a visitor leaving with a clear sense that SecurBank is a step-change above what they currently have, and a low-friction path to starting.

## Positioning

Banking at the speed and precision of the markets it serves.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA**: Explore Products — the site architecture leads with product depth and rate specificity, allowing visitors to qualify themselves through discovery before any commitment is asked
- **Secondary CTA**: Apply Now — surfaces once the visitor has engaged with a specific product and seen proof of its terms
- **The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds**: "This is the bank built for people whose money is always working."
- **Belief ladder**: (1) SecurBank is serious — not a challenger bank, not a legacy institution; (2) The products are genuinely superior to what I have — rates, access, tools; (3) Getting started is not a bureaucratic wall
- **Proof on hand**: Rate specificity (9.99% variable APR, £0 annual fee), instant-approval positioning, UK/EU regulatory alignment (SECURE KYC, UK/EU SUPPORT), institutional vocabulary throughout

## Brand Personality

**Authoritative · Kinetic · Rare.** The brand does not whisper institutional confidence — it radiates it. Hanken Grotesk at heavy weight, obsidian-to-surface depth layers, primary cyan as the single electric signal. The design system is called "Kinetic Trust / Neon Glass" and the name is the brief: structural precision with live energy, glass-surface depth without decorative excess. Copy is direct and precise — institutional vocabulary, never retail-speak, never jargon for its own sake.

## Anti-references

- **Traditional private banking** (Coutts, J.P. Morgan Private Bank): serifed restraint, dark wood aesthetic, old-money discretion. Reads as slow and exclusionary. SecurBank is not inherited wealth — it is earned performance.
- **Bloomberg / Reuters terminal**: grey grid density, information-first with zero visual intention. Functional but not aspirational. SecurBank has the same data rigour with a completely different visual register.
- **Neobank / fintech-lite** (Monzo, Revolut consumer): candy-bright pastels, rounded-everything, playful microcopy. Too retail, too thin. SecurBank is a serious institution.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the brand.** Every interaction reinforces "speed and precision." Sluggish transitions, loading spinners in prominent positions, or heavy page weight are directly off-brand — they contradict the positioning claim.
2. **Discovery before commitment.** The architecture assumes the visitor is qualified but unconvinced. Lead with product range, rate specificity, and capability proof before surfacing any form. A visitor who finds the Apply Now button before they're ready bounces; one who finds it after they've explored converts.
3. **Rare is earned, not asserted.** The neon-glass design system — obsidian depth, primary cyan, Hanken Grotesk at 900 weight — signals rarity through restraint and precision, not through claiming it. Every pattern that dilutes this (template cards, identical grids, generic eyebrows on every section) directly weakens the signal.
4. **Institutional vocabulary, always.** Copy, labels, and error messages use the language of finance — not the language of consumer apps. "Variable APR" not "rate." "Access" not "get." "Portfolio" not "account overview."
5. **Glass is a system, not a decoration.** Glassmorphism is the site's structural visual language and is used purposefully for cards, overlays, and hero surfaces only. Any additional blur layer that doesn't carry informational weight is noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: **WCAG 2.1 AAA**. The institutional audience includes users with visual impairments, motor disabilities, and assistive technology dependencies. Full keyboard navigability, ARIA labelling on all interactive elements, minimum 7:1 contrast for body text, and `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all transitions. Screen reader accessibility on all data tables and form inputs is non-negotiable.
